The trends of child malnutrition

Data set was published by UNICEF/ WHO/World Bank Group.

The forms/ categories of malnutrition highlighted in children less than 5 years old: Stunting, overweight, wasting and underweight. The first question that I was interested in was, ‘Which category of malnutrition will have the total highest number of cases?’ On the first line graph and bar graph, it showed stunting group is the highest amongst those categories. It approximated 5 millions of children under 5 years old suffered stunting problems. Stunting is a severe malnutrition and children who are stunted falls below the median height-for-age of the World Health Organization’s Child Growth Standards. Stunting can be caused by nutritional intake of the child, as well as the mother during pregnancy, the recurrence of infectious diseases and infections from poor hygiene practices.

  • Date: December 2016
  • Client: Data Exploration & Visualization Class
  • Category: Data Visualization using Spotfire
